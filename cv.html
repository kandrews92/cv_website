Research Experience

05/2015-Present   Graduate Research Assistant, Nano Fabrication and Electron Transport Laboratory, Wayne State University, Detroit, MI, USA
    Current ongoing research with collaborators at Michigan State University (Dr. David Tomanek) and Oak Ridge National Laboratory (Dr. David Mandrus). Project is focused on producing high-quality two-dimensional nano-devices using transition-metal dichalcogenides (for example MoS\(_2\) and WSe\(_2\)). In these devices we measure the transport properties and seek ways to improve and optimize device performance for application to low-power digital electronics.
01/2014-05/2014   Researcher, International Course on Computational Physics (ICCP), Technische Universiteit Delft, Delft, Netherlands and Michigan State University, MI, USA
    A joint collaboration between Michigan State University and Technische Universiteit Delft in Delft, Netherlands concerned with developing and implementing numerical technical to stochastic processes. Using Monte Carlo simulations and various other differential equation solving methods models were developed for polymer chain growth, molecular dynamics, and quantum particle interactions for complex systems.
01/2013-12/2013   Undergraduate Research Assistant, Neutron Star Evolution and Developmental Limits, Department of Physics and Astronomy, Michigan State University, East Lansing, MI
    Project for undergraduate senior thesis involiving the implementation of methods to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation for simple neutron stars to obtain the mass-limit and analyze the overall evolution of neutron stars as a function of initial mass density. In addition to using standard coding techniques, this project also implemented the open-source stellar evolution software library Modules for Experiments in Stellar Astrophysics (MESA) and the use of databases from the Joint Institute of Nuclear Astrophysics (JINA).
05/2012-06/2013   Undergraduate Research Assistant, High Resolution Array Group (HIRA): SAMURAI-TPC Project National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory, Michigan State University, East Lansing, MI, USA
    An international collaboration with several universities in the U.S., Japan, and Germany of experimentalists and theoreticians. Project goal is to understand the effects of constraints on the symmetry energy in the nuclear equation of state over a range of densities, specifically near the range of densities consistent with neutron stars.
